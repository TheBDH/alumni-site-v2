Herald History

The Herald began publishing daily in 1891 and is an independent, 501(c) non-profit corporation, and has been financially independent from Brown since 1975.

The inaugural four-page issue of The Brown Daily Herald was published over 130 years ago. On Wednesday, Dec. 2, 1891, The Herald's editors described their intentions in undertaking the sixth daily college newspaper in the country with gravitas:

"This issue begins the career of the Brown Daily Herald. Whether it shall remain as one of the fixtures of Brown or die a natural death is left to the students themselves. ... The Herald will not lay claim to any literary merit but will contain only the college news, notices, and announcements that may be of daily interest to college men. ... We ask a fair trial and we will demonstrate our right to live."

Current Herald Initiatives

The Herald’s commitment to providing insightful, timely news to the Brown community has remained steadfast. Here are some of the exciting changes we've implemented recently:

  • New Office Space: In 2019, The Herald office has found a new home at 88 Benevolent St. after moving from 195 Angell. Our new office is not just a change of scenery — it has allowed us to better serve our readers and enhance our operational efficiency.
  • Expanded Digital Reach:
    • Newsletter: Our daily weekday newsletter as well as our once-a-week newsletters recapping top stories of the week, sports headliners, or what’s been buzzing on campus ensure you never miss out on important updates and exclusive features.
    • Instagram: Follow us on Instagram for visual storytelling, community highlights and more interactive content.
    • BDH App: Coming soon, the BDH app will provide a seamless reading experience for users on-the-go, with personalized content recommendations and real-time news updates.
  • Innovative Tech Team: Our developers are at the forefront of transforming how news is delivered. One of their key projects includes working with the data from the Herald’s semesterly poll, which surveys students on some of the most important happenings on campus and in the country.
